Artificial Intelligence History

The concept of artificial intelligence (AI) can be traced back to ancient times, where philosophers pondered questions related to life and autonomy. Inventors in ancient times created mechanical devices called automatons, which moved independently without human intervention. The term "automaton" originated from ancient Greek and referred to acting of one's own will. One early example is a mechanical pigeon built by a friend of Plato in 400 BCE. However, in this article, we will primarily focus on the advancements made in the 20th century by engineers and scientists towards the development of modern AI.

AI Voice Assistance

The rise of voice assistants and smart speaker devices has been a significant trend in recent years. However, voice assistants have a longer history that predates the popularization of Amazon Echo. Starting in the early 1960s, voice assistants began to emerge, and a timeline showcases the evolution of the voice revolution. The timeline reveals four distinct eras in the history of voice assistants. The Origin period marks the beginning, with IBM introducing the Shoebox device, which had limited capabilities but could understand a limited vocabulary of 16 words and 9 digits.
